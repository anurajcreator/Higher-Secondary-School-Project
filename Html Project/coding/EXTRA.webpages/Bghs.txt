	    Ballygunge Government High School, or BGHS, is a school in West Bengal, India. This is a boys only school for secondary and higher secondary level students. Its medium of instruction is English . It was once considered one of the best Bengali medium schools in Kolkata and continues to be a good one. The campus includes a fairly large play-ground and the school offers variety of extracurricular activities for its students. The school has produced many well known academics, film and theater personalities, journalist and musicians. Currently, there are over 1200 students enrolled at this school. The school is adjacent to Kolkata Regional Transport Office (RTO)/Ballygunge police station and Kolkata Motor Vehicles Department<br><br>
	    
	 	<h3>Classes</h3>
		Classes between class one to class five are taught in the morning section, while classes between six and twelve are conducted at the day section.

		<h3>Events</h3>
		<ul type="circle">
			<li>Wall magazine "Iiha" with articles, poems and short stories.</li>
			<li>School magazine "Baagbitan" is published every year. Students and teachers contribute poems, essays, short stories, and sketches.</li>
			<li>An annual three-day arts fair is held at the time of Saraswati Puja. The students contribute crafts, drawings, scientific experiments and works for public display.</li>
			<li>Sports events are run during the annual sports.</li>
			<li>Independence day is celebrated with a ceremony every year on 15 August in the school premises.</li>
			<li>The school's foundation day is celebrated on 3 January.</li>
			<li>Saraswati Puja is one of the main festivals held in the school.</li>
			<li>Activities include theater, singing, drawing competition, debate, and recitation.</li>
		</ul>
